I am a postdoctoral researcher of mathematics at the LAGA Laboratoire, Université Sorbonne Paris Nord, and in IMJ-PRG at Université Paris-Cité. I am part of the group of Yonatan Harpaz. Before that I was a postdoc at the University of Zurich, in Joseph Ayoub's research group. Even before, I was a PhD student at the University of Duisburg-Essen in Marc Levine's group.

My interests lie around interactions between homotopy theory and algebraic geometry, in particular within the setting of motivic homotopy theory.
I am interested in information one can gather from different motivic invariants for the study of singularities. In my research I combine the machinery of nearby cycles with the study of invariants for schemes lying in the Grothendieck-Witt ring of the base field; these invariants refine integer-valued invariants of enumerative geometry.
    Some projects I'm working on:
  • A paper based on my PhD thesis, in which I prove a local formula refining an algebraic-geomteric version of the Milnor formula on singularities (Journal of Algebra, 2025).
  • A preprint on defining motivic Euler classes for singular varieties.
  • A work in progress with Emil Jacobsen on the Picard Lefschetz formula for motives.
  • A work in progress with Felipe Espreafico Guelerman about motivic and real DT invariants.
  • A work in progress with Niels Feld, Yonatan Harpaz, Tasos Moulinos, and Simon Pepin Lehalleur, on trace formula for singularity categories with duality.
  • A work in progress with Stephen McKean, Anubhav Nanavaty, and Jesse Pajwani, on motivic invariants of morphism pairs (a one level higher version of the quadratic Euler characteristic)
