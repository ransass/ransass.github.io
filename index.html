Before that I was a postdoc at the University of Zurich, in Joseph Ayoub's research group. Even before, I was a PhD student at the University of Duisburg-Essen in Marc Levine's group.

My interests lie around interactions between homotopy theory and algebraic geometry, in particular within the setting of motivic homotopy theory.
I am interested in information one can gather from different motivic invariants for the study of singularities. In my research I combine the machinery of nearby cycles with the study of invariants for schemes lying in the Grothendieck-Witt ring of the base field; these invariants refine integer-valued invariants of enumerative geometry.
    Some projects:
  • A preprint based on my PhD thesis, in which I prove a local formula refining an algebraic-geomteric version of the Milnor formula on singularities.
  • A preprint on defining motivic Euler classes for singular varieties.
  • A work in progress with Emil Jacobsen is on the Picard Lefschetz Theorem for motives.
  • A work in progress with Yonatan Harpaz, Tasos Moulinos, and others, on Hermitian trace formulas for singularity categories.
